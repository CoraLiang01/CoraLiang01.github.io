About me

I am a PhD student at Shanghai Jiao Tong University[cite: 7], majoring in Management Science and Engineering with a deep passion for academic research in operations management. During my master's study at Shanghai University of Finance and Economics, I was ranked first in my class with a GPA of 3.98/4.00 [cite: 12] and was recognized with prestigious awards, including the National Scholarship [cite: 66] and accolades in the National Undergraduate Mathematical Contest in Modeling[cite: 67]. Notably, my first research paper is under review at the INFORMS Journal on Computing, underscoring the computational and practical contributions of my work. Beyond academics, I have embraced teaching assistant roles [cite: 27] and gained industry experience through internships [cite: 60], as well as engaging in entrepreneurship[cite: 53], where I applied my analytical and strategic skills to build and grow a self-started business. I am passionate about my doctoral studies and am eager to further deepen my expertise, contributing to innovative solutions in the field of operations management.

Research Projects

  1. A Decomposition Framework for Customized Supply-Chain Decisions, 03.2023-Present
    Supervisor: DongdongGe; Zizhuo Wang (IJOC under review)

    • Formulated a third-party logistics company’s integrated decision problem as a multi-period mixed-integer program to minimize total costs under complex coupling constraints. [cite: 35]

    • Proposed a large-scale acceleration framework incorporating column generation combined with machine learning, sweeping, and column selection techniques for the linear relaxation problem. [cite: 36, 37]

    • Designed a specific rounding algorithm to recover the integer optimal solution. Guaranteed a near-optimal solution within a 0.7% optimal gap and increased solution speed by 66% compared to the benchmark on both synthetic and real-world datasets. [cite: 37, 38]

  2. Joint Acceptance and Fulfillment Policy in an Omni-channel Environment, 12.2023-07.2024 [cite: 40]
    Supervisor: Chaolin Yang (Working Paper) [cite: 39]

    • Proposed a two-stage stochastic decision framework including online acceptance and fulfillment for the omnichannel retailer based on the ”buy-online ship-from-store” pattern. [cite: 41]

    • Derived local and global threshold policies for accepting orders and provided theoretical guarantees, including preserving quasi-convexity properties of the expected costs in a multi-period setting. [cite: 42, 43]

    • Employed the IPA algorithm and utilized the dual information to demonstrate sub-optimal threshold settings and solve the fulfillment problem within polynomial time. [cite: 44]

  3. End-to-End Algorithm Implementation for the Full-Link Intelligent Supply Chain, 10.2021-09.2022 [cite: 46, 47]
    Supervisor: Dongdong Ge (Outstanding Graduation Thesis) [cite: 45]

    • Implemented end-to-end innovative optimization algorithms to solve the demand prediction, inventory management, and replenishment planning in supply chain management. [cite: 48]

    • Developed a multivariate hierarchical time-series structure and MES LSTM model for large-scale demand forecasts, achieving 72% to 97% prediction accuracy and a 10% reduction in RMSE. [cite: 49]

    • Designed the customized service level, resulting in a 16% reduction in safety stock levels, and ran simulations for soft constraint penalties. [cite: 50]
