Research Projects

  1. A Decomposition Framework for Customized Supply-Chain Decisions, 03.2023-Present

    • Formulated a third-party logistics company’s integrated decision problem problem as a multi-period mixed-integer program with the objective to minimize total costs under complex coupling constraints.

    • Proposed a large-scale acceleration framework incorporating column generation combined with machine learning, the sweeping and column selection techniques for the linear relaxation problem.

    • Designed a specific rounding algorithm to recover the integer optimal solution. Guaranteed a nearoptimal solution within a 1% optimal gap and increased solution speed by 70% compared to the benchmark on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

  2. Joint Acceptance and Fulfillment Policy in an Omni-channel Environment, 01.2022-12.2022

    • Proposed a two-stage stochastic decision framework including online acceptance and fulfillment for the omni-channel retailer based on the ”buy-online ship-from-store” pattern.

    • Derived local and global threshold policies for accepting orders and provide theoretical guarantees including the preservation of quasi-convexity properties of the expected costs in a multi-period setting.

    • Employed the IPA algorithm and utilize the dual information to demonstrate sub-optimal threshold settings and solve the fulfillment problem within polynomial time.

  3. End-to-End Algorithm Implementation for the Full-Link Intelligent Supply Chain., 01.2021-12.2021

    • Implemented end-to-end innovative optimization algorithms to solve the demand prediction, inventory management, and replenishment planning in supply chain management.

    • Developed a multivariate hierarchical time-series structure and MES LSTM model for large-scale demand forecasts, achieving 72% to 97% prediction accuracy and a 10% reduction in RMSE.

    • Designed the customized service level resulting in a 16% reduction in safety stock levels and run simulations for soft constraint penalties.

  4. Acceleration Algorithms for Cardinality Constrained Portfolio Optimization, 03.2021-12.2021

    • Solved the carnality-constrained mean-variance portfolio optimization problem, which is generally NP-hard. Proposed several heuristic methods, namely, the continuous-relaxation based method the l1 -norm based solution, the integer programming based solution and the SDP based solution.

    • Evaluated the efficient and the accuracy of the proposed methods over the commercial MIQP solver on the real-life stock data and the simulated data sets.
